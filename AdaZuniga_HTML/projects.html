Projects

Doish:

Right after graduating from college, I decided to start a new project by myself, so I choose to design and develop Doish.
Doish is an android app that slashes time consumed tracking everyday activities avoiding daily toil.
The Java-based application developed using Android studio granted me additional practice with object-oriented programming concepts as well as basic knowledge of what it is like to develop a smartphone app.

Automated Tracking App:

Tracing daily computer actions provided in sightful data analysis for identifying ways in which business processes may be automatically evaluated to identify opportunities where automation technologies may be applied or where the process may be simplified. This C# based application slashed the time to document processes and where potential efficiencies and savings may be realized. Effectively, automation leveraging automation.
This project was quite unique since it was already halfway done. However, I am glad o got to work with it since it forced me to understand how simple and important it is to write comments on your code, how sometimes we can not change things so we need to work with what we have because at the end of the day our job is to fully understand and deliver a solution to a given problem.

Front end:

I have always had an artistic side on everything I do so programming was not going to be an exception. During college I took a variety of courses that focused on an area of software development but, I learned to focus on computer imaging. I really enjoyed the design part of those computer imaging classes so I wanted to take my learning further and decided to take a UX class during my last semester of college. My user interaction class just gave me a brief introduction to how powerful front end development is, so I decided to take it further and learn more about the different faces of front end development.
This website was designed and developed with the purpose of practising front end development all the way from creating site maps, wireframes, design (and having a responsive design), development, UI, Webhosting, domains and databases.
